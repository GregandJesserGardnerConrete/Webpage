Frequently Asked Questions In Minneapolis, MN

At Greg and Jesse Gardner Concrete Contractors, we understand that many of our valued customers in the metro area and surrounding communities have questions about concrete. Our team always takes the time to answer your questions and to provide you with reliable service; however, there are some frequently asked questions that many of our clients and customers share.

We've put together this FAQ page so that we can better serve you. If you do not see the questions you have about concrete installation and repair work here, let us answer your questions personally. Please call us directly, we're happy to help in any way we can.

1. Are you licensed or insured?

Yes, we carry workers' comp and licensed, bonded, Liability Insurance.

2. Do you install any sub-base preparation for the concrete projects?

Yes, we use a granular base underneath before concrete placement and compact it with a mechanical plate tamper.

3. Do you reinforce the concrete?

Yes, we install rebar for primary reinforcement in the concrete. We also use fiberglass rebar for supporting the surrounding concrete around pools.

4. How thick do you pour concrete for my project?

5. Do you use a concrete sealer for my new concrete?

Yes, we use a triple action sealer initially, which: A. Cures your concrete, B. Includes Penetrating Silane Seal, and C. also includes an Acrylic Membrane Seal as well. Only exceptions for same-day sealing application would include decorative stamped finishes and exposed aggregate finishes. We would return to seal these projects for an initial seal.

6. How long will my concrete last?

Concrete can last a long time and gets stronger over time. Just monitor and control salt exposure and call us for a return visit to apply a re-coat application of concrete sealer when necessary.

7. What forms of payment do you accept?

We accept cash, check (personal, bank-certified, or other bank checks), and PayPal.

8. What other services do you offer?

Retaining walls, pavers, footings, and new block foundation for additions and basement wall repair.

Core fills, glass block window install, window wells, exterior grading around foundation.

Parging interior or exterior to scaling foundation walls and also drain tile.

Stained or colored concrete.

Power washing concrete or paver surfaces and resealing.
